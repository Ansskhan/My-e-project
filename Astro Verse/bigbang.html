The Big Bang Theory – How It All Began

Imagine rewinding time back to the very beginning—before stars, planets, or even space itself existed. The Big Bang Theory is the leading explanation of how our universe started. About 13.8 billion years ago, everything we see today was squeezed into a tiny, unimaginably dense and hot point, called a singularity. Then, in an instant, it expanded—not like an explosion, but more like a balloon inflating.

What is big bang theory?

The Big Bang Theory explains the origin of the universe. Around 13.8 billion years ago, the universe began as a tiny, dense, and hot point, then rapidly expanded. This expansion continues today, shaping galaxies, stars, and planets. Scientists support this theory with evidence like cosmic microwave background radiation and the expanding universe. Conclusion

  • Scientifically:
    • The Big Bang Theory is the most widely accepted explanation for the origin of the universe. It states that the universe began around 13.8 billion years ago from an extremely hot, dense point and has been expanding ever since. Evidence such as cosmic microwave background radiation, redshift of galaxies, and abundance of light elements support this theory. However, while the Big Bang explains the origin and expansion of the universe, it does not address what existed before it or what caused it—questions that remain open for scientific exploration.

  • In Quran Perspective:
    • The Qur'an (21:30) states that the heavens and the earth were once a "closed-up mass" before being "opened out," a description that aligns with the Big Bang concept. The Quran does not provide a scientific formula but offers a broader insight into creation, emphasizing that the universe was brought into existence by divine will. Additionally, the Quran repeatedly encourages reflection on the cosmos, nature, and life, showing that science and faith are not necessarily in conflict but can complement each other.
