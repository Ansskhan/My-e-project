Comets

Comets – The Wandering Stars of Our Solar System

Comets have amazed people for a long time. These bright, mysterious streaks in the night sky are often called “cosmic snowballs.” They’re some of the oldest objects in our solar system and can tell us a lot about how it all began. Unlike planets, comets have long, stretched-out orbits, taking hundreds or even thousands of years to go around the Sun.

What are comets and how they formed?

Comets are small space objects made of ice, rock, and dust. They mostly come from two places: the Kuiper Belt and the Oort Cloud, which are way out in the solar system. When a comet gets close to the Sun, the heat makes its icy core turn into gas, creating a glowing cloud called a coma. This gas and dust get pushed away by the Sun’s energy, forming a long, beautiful tail that we can see from Earth.

Parts of a Comet

    A comet has three main parts:

  • Nucleus:
    The solid, frozen center made of ice, rock, and some organic stuff.
  • Coma:
    The cloud of gas and dust that forms around the nucleus when it warms up.
  • Tail:
    The glowing streak of dust and gas that always points away from the Sun because of solar energy.

According to Quran

The Quran mentions celestial phenomena that some interpret as references to comets and meteors. For instance, it describes meteors as missiles used by angels against jinns, and there are verses that suggest the presence of ice in the heavens, which some link to comets. These interpretations highlight the Quran's connection to cosmic events.

Famous Coments

Many comets have made a big impact on human history. One of the most famous is Halley’s Comet, which shows up about every 76 years and has been seen since ancient times. Another well-known comet is Hale-Bopp, which lit up the sky in 1997 and amazed people everywhere. More recently, Comet NEOWISE was a stunning sight in 2020, visible to anyone who looked up.
